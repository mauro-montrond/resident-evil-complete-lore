Natural History.

In the 19th century, Henry Travis found a rare flower in west africa in the kijuju region known as the stairway of the sun. Legends say that whoever consumed the flower and survived its effects was granted superhuman abilities.

Dark Ambitions.

In the 1960s, three virologists and longtime friends, Edward Ashford, Ozwell E. Spencer and James Marcus rediscovered the flowers in West Africa. Spencer had plans of his own where he had the vision to remake the world creating a superior race of humans and make himself as his ruler.

Patient Zero.

Upon the completion of the mansion in 1967, Spencer realised that Trevor was aware of the laboratory and knew too much and began making plans for disposing him. Trevor was busy with work at the time and sends his wife and daughter, Jessica and Lisa. Jessica and Lisa suffer as test subjects. Spencer's scientists creats two variations of the Progenitor Virus.

Umbrella Corporation.

In 1968, Ozwell E. Spencer creates the Umbrella Corporation which would initially operate in the pharmaceutical sector with his headquarters established in Europe. A Umbrella Executive Training Facility is established in Raccoon City, in the Arklay Mountains, to train scientists and special employees for Umbrella and Marcus as its director. Soon after the creation of Umbrella, Edward Ashford dies due to exposure of the Progenitor Virus.

Project Code Veronica.

Edward's son, Alexander Ashford, continues his father's research and uses his knowledge in genetics engineering and orders the construction of a Antartic Installation, where he would work in a private project without Spencer awareness called Project Code: Veronica. He fertilizes an egg with various genetic improvements in the attempt of cloning the matriarch Veronica Ashford. The Project in concluded in 1971 and two twins are born: Alexia and Alfred.

Spencer Secret Project.

Spencer begins his secret research inside of Umbrella named Project W (or Project Wesker), where hundred of children born from parents with "superior intellect" would be collected for the experiment. Years later, the Wesker Children, received a new dose of an prototype virus based in the Progenitor. Many died but among the few survivors is a young boy called Albert Wesker .

The Prodigies.

James Marcus while working obsessively on the Progenitor Research in the executive training facility, entered in disagreement with Spencer due to his work not bringing results and making high expenses to the company. In September 1977, 2 young scientists joined the training facility research team: William Birkin and Albert Wesker. They are hired to trained virology as personal assistents of James Marcus.

T-Virus.

In September 1978, Marcus finally was able to create a stable variant of the Progenitor, he names it Tyrant virus or T-virus for short, which was capable of causing mutation in a cellular level. Spencer finds out about the discovery and was able to acquire a sample which was later sent to the European division for studies.
